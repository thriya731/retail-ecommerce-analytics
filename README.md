# retail-ecommerce-analytics
Thriya | Aspiring Data Analyst
Overview
An end-to-end analytics project built to mirror what a real business analyst actually does — messy data, real decisions, and insights that mean something. The dataset covers 3,015 orders, 500 customers, and 120 products across 2022–2024.

Tools Used
Python, pandas, numpy, matplotlib, seaborn, scikit-learn, Power BI, DAX

What I Did
1.Cleaned the data - dealt with duplicate rows, mixed date formats, inconsistent casing, missing values, and discount errors. Not a clean Kaggle dataset. Real issues that break real pipelines.
2.Analysed it properly - revenue trends, customer segmentation, product profitability, cohort retention, and channel concentration risk. The kind of work that actually informs business decisions.
3.Found things that matter - Lapsed customers spend the most per order but are being lost. 34% of Premium customers are drifting toward churn. Discounting isn't driving volume the way most businesses assume. Revenue rank and profit rank tell completely different stories.
4.Built a Power BI report - 5 pages, DAX measures, slicers, conditional formatting heatmap. Something a stakeholder could actually use.
5.Tried predictive modelling - built a churn classifier. Got 100% accuracy, which sounds great until you realise it means data leakage. Documented it anyway because understanding why a model is wrong matters more than a perfect score.

Key Findings
1.34% of Premium customers are quietly drifting toward churn - still active, but the clock is ticking. 
2.Lapsed customers spend the most per order of any segment, yet the business is losing them. 
3.Top revenue products and top profit products are rarely the same list, and the gap between them is where margin is being lost. 
4.Three product categories depend on a single sales channel for over 25% of their revenue - one campaign failure away from a significant dip, and perhaps most counterintuitively, no-discount orders drive more volume than any discounted tier.
